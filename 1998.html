1998

Teaching
In 1998 Stuart was unit chair, or supervised project students in the following units:

  • SEB121 on- and off-campus - approx 120 students, lectures, tutorials and assessment;
  • SEB421 on- and off-campus - approx 45 students, all lectures, tutorials and assessment;
  • SEJ341/342 - supervision and assessment of one BTech student project;
  • SEJ343/443 - supervision and assessment of four BEng student projects;
  • SEJ401 - unit chair;
  • SEJ601 - unit chair;
  • SEN713 - supervision and assessment of one MTech student project; and
  • DTech - co-supervision of one Doctor of Technology candidate.

In 1998 Stuart revised the unit course material for SEB421.

All class lecture and tutorial notes (including solutions to review questions and past papers) were made available to students electronically via the Internet. Extensive use was made of the Web for the provision of supplementary information to on- and off-campus students.

Stuart was nominated for one of the Vice Chancellor's Awards for Oustanding Achievement.

Research
Stuart worked on his Doctoral program.

At the First Annual Unesco International Centre for Engineering Education Conference in Melbourne, Stuart presented the paper, Responses of Final-year Engineering Students to Assessment Tasks Designed to Develop Generic Professional Practice Skills.  This paper was lead paper for the conference session on 'Effective Methods in Education and Training'.

At the Seventh Annual Australasian Conference of Engineering Management Educators in Melbourne, Stuart presented the paper, Considerations in the Design of Undergraduate Engineering Management Programs for Flexible Learning.

At the Tenth Annual Convention and Conference of the Australasian Association for Engineering Education in Gladstone, Stuart presented the paper, Mature Age Engineering Students' Perceptions of Engineering Management.

Stuart investigated the computer usage patterns of both on- and off-campus undergraduate engineering students, the application of Web-based conceptual maps to aid engineering management education and the perceptions of engineering management education of mature-age engineering students.

Stuart was awarded a $10,000 grant from the James N Kirby Foundation to develop multimedia resources to be used in engineering and technology education is primary schools.
