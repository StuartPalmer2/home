Engineering the Future: Preparing Professional Engineers for the 21st Century

Lloyd, B E, Ferguson, C, Palmer, S and Rice, MR, Engineering the Future: Preparing Professional Engineers for the 21st Century, A4, 200 pp., published by APESMA in association with Histec Publications, May 2001.

The book looks behind the historical development of events and issues that have shaped the engineering profession in Australia within the context of the historical, social and global development of the profession. The analysis leads to a wide-ranging discussion on the future of the Australian engineering profession.

Lloyd is the principal contributor on concepts of professionalism and on future needs for effective practice of engineering. As a Past President of the Institution of Engineers, Australia, directly responsible for many initiatives of the Engineering team, he is a well seasoned researcher in this area.

Chapters by Rice focus on statistical analysis of engineer populations and projections, to provide significant insights into the national and global environment in which engineers work. Rice is the acknowledged Australian expert on these matters.

Recognising the increasing significance of continuing professional development, the book also includes chapters by Ferguson and Palmer on new developments in engineering education. Ferguson discusses the new development of graduate attributes, an approach that is changing the focus of engineering education, and considers the broadening range of teaching strategies that need to be employed. He also discusses issues involved in distance education. Palmer gives particular focus to the teaching of engineering management in engineering undergraduate courses, a question still neglected by many engineering schools.

The book goes well beyond academic interests, and is of value for all engineers interested in maintaining their profession. There are strong messages for IEAust concerning the future relevance of the Institution to the profession. Those who work in fields associated with the engineering profession also will find compelling reading in the book.
