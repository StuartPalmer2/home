Using text analytics to visualise international engineering postgraduate student challenges

CONTEXT

The spread of Coronavirus disease has left public universities counting the cost of the Australian Government’s decision to close borders. Schools of Engineering across Australia, saw a rapid decline in revenue in 2020-2021 and their sustainability and over reliance on income from international students has come under question. A knee-jerk reaction has been to conceptualise new program models or delivery approaches. However, this alone does not go far enough in addressing the sustainability question.

PURPOSE OR GOAL

There are three fundamental questions that need to be addressed to unpack what it means to develop a sustainable postgraduate engineering program: the cost of the degree, the overall student experience and graduate outcomes. While Australia’s skilled migration policy can have an influence in international student interest in postgraduate coursework degrees beyond 2022, it is also important to consider student perspectives to design such sustainable programs. As international students were only included in the Quality Indicators for Learning and Teaching surveys 2016-2017, their experiences have only come to the fore recently and does not fully represent the cohort experience.

APPROACH OR METHODOLOGY/METHODS

Our mixed method study, which uses students as partners in the data collection and analysis process, explored student motivations, aspirations and expectations from a master’s degree in engineering. We presented outcomes from our study focussed on the quantitative aspects of the data (Krishnan, Campanale & Palmer, 2020). The investigation also collected qualitative data in the form of open-ended text-based response from student participants to a range of questions related to their motivations for, and experiences of, study in Australia. Here we use text analysis and visualisation to identify and explore the themes present in the students’ responses to the study’s prompting questions.

ACTUAL OR ANTICIPATED OUTCOMES

In this paper, we present our findings from looking deeply into what international students want from coursework master’s degrees and by asking the questions about what it means to support their engagement in learning and what it means to nurture their development. The open-ended text responses from students to five prompting questions included more than 2600 words. Co-occurrence network visualisations were produced for each of the response text sets to assist in the interpretation of the student responses.

CONCLUSIONS/RECOMMENDATIONS/SUMMARY

In this paper we present the motivations, aspirations, expectations and perceptions of international students in a postgraduate coursework engineering degree and seek to contribute to the development of strategies that can have a positive impact on their learning for professional employment. We believe that critical to improving graduate employability is providing students with multiple and diverse Work Integrated Learning (WIL) opportunities ‘within or beyond the formal curriculum’ to bridge the cognitive gap in their understanding for how their educational experience will prepare them for professional work. The text analytics method presented may be a useful tool for others undertaking similar evaluations of student experiences based on text data from surveys, interviews or focus groups.
